<p><p>The Monocrystalline Diamond market is primarily driven by two production methods: HPHT (High Pressure High Temperature) and CVD (Chemical Vapor Deposition). HPHT diamonds are created by mimicking natural diamond formation under extreme pressure and heat, yielding high-quality stones often used in jewelry. CVD diamonds are grown in controlled environments using gas, producing purer and more customizable diamonds, ideal for industrial and technological applications. Both methods cater to distinct market segments, with HPHT focusing on gemstones and CVD on precision-driven industries.</p></p>
